Connectivity · the rural tail

Where fiber stops being worth it, satellite is the floor.

Satellite isn't competing with fiber — it's the floor under the part of the map fiber can't afford to reach. Fiber's cost-per-location explodes as density falls and crosses above Starlink's flat ≈$600 + $120/mo somewhere in the rural tail. That tail — the FCC's ~4% unserved, almost all rural — is structurally Starlink's.

Honest caveats — what keeps this defensible

Capacity is the binding risk
Flat cost assumes the network absorbs load; as subs head toward 25M, congestion (not coverage) is the threat until V3 satellites scale.
